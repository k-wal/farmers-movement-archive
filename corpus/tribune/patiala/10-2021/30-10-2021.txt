30-10-2021||Ravneet Singh||Patiala: Man who faked his death held on murder charge||Tribune News ServicePatiala, October 29The Patiala police have cracked a case of honour killing and arrested two persons, including a woman, in this connection.Patiala SSP HS Bhullar said accused Gurinder Singh had murdered his cousin Harneet Kaur (19) last year and then killed another man Varinder Singh (32) on October 4 this year. Gurinder had faked his death after murdering his cousin and remained underground for a year.He also wanted to kill two other persons related to the case, before which he was nabbed on October 28.The SSP in a press conference on Friday said the police had cracked the murder case of Varinder Singh (Bania) of Bosar Kalan village, who was shot dead on October 4.He said during investigation, the police found that the murder of Harneet Kaur, which took place at Bolar Kalan village on October 12 last year, was also committed by Gurinder Singh.“Gurinder had murdered his cousin 16 days before her marriage with Sehajpreet and then faked his own suicide,” the SSP said.He said Gurinder also prepared a fake suicide note stating that he was unhappy with his cousin’s alleged friendship with Sehajpreet, who was the brother-in-law of Varinder. The latter had been trying to get Sehajpreet and Harneet Kaur married.Police officials said during investigation they found that Gurinder, who had faked his death, was in fact alive. He left Patiala and started living in Delhi and MP. A team of CIA staff, led by inspector Shaminder Singh, nabbed Gurinder from the Devigarh-Patiala road and registered a case under Section 302 of the IPC and the Arms Act on October 28.The SSP said the accused confessed to have murdered Varinder and was also planning to murder two other persons.The police have recovered pistols from the possession of Gurinder and also nabbed a woman, Manjit Kaur (40), who helped him in stealing the weapons used in the crimes. The SSP said the police had solved five murder cases in the past 14 days and officials, who were involved in cracking the cases would be honoured.||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/patiala/patiala-man-who-faked-his-death-held-on-murder-charge-331857
30-10-2021||Patiala||Covid warriors protest, demand regular jobs||Outsourced Covid-19 warriors, paramedical staff and nurses, of Government Rajindra Hospital today staged a protest and blocked Chandigarh-Bathinda national highway outside the hospital demanding regularisation of their jobs. They are said to have been told by the authorities that their contract has already been over in the month of September. However, following the assurance by the minister to continue their job, they are still performing their duties.The protesting employees were supported by farmer and labour unions. They demanded immediate regularisation of their jobs. The protesters said they were hired last year during an emergency situation. Each and every healthcare worker has worked around-the-clock to provide medical services during the Covid waves, the protesters added.Amanjot Singh, a nurse, said, “We suffered personal losses while working in Covid wards. The government should immediately give us jobs.”Dr HS Rekhi, Medical Superintendent, Government Rajindra Hospital, said their cases had already been taken up by higher authorities, however, they were still adamant to continue their protest. — TNS||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/patiala/covid-warriors-protest-demand-regular-jobs-331858
