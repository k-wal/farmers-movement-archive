29-01-2021||Srinagar||Mercury plunges as  cold wave grips Valley||Mercury plunged by several degrees across Kashmir as the Valley was gripped by intense cold wave conditions on Thursday, officials said here. The extreme subzero temperatures have resulted in freezing of water bodies and drinking water supply lines in many areas of the Valley. Srinagar city recorded a low of -5.6°C, plunging more than three degrees from -2°C the previous night, the officials said. Qazigund recorded a minimum of -10.5°C — down from -4.4°C the previous night, they said. The minimum temperature in Gulmarg settled at -13.4°C last night — down from last night’s -12°C.
