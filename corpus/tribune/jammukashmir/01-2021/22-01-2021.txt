22-01-2021||Srinagar||Mercury dips across Kashmir Valley||The intense cold wave conditions continued unabated in Jammu and Kashmir on Thursday as the minimum temperature stayed several degrees below the freezing point.Srinagar city recorded a low of -7°C, down from -6°C the previous night, a Met Department official said.The minimum temperature in the city last night was nearly five degrees below the normal for this time of the year, he said.The Gulmarg skiing resort in north Kashmir’s Baramulla district recorded -7.8°C last night, down from -6.5°C the night earlier.Pahalgam tourist resort recorded a low of -9.3°C, down from the previous night’s -8.3°C, the Meteorological Department said.Qazigund — the gateway town to the Valley — recorded a minimum of 8.8°C, it said.Kupwara in north Kashmir recorded a low of -5.9°C, while Kokernag recorded -6.9°C.Several water bodies in the Kashmir valley have frozen due to the bone-chilling cold.A thick layer of ice has formed over several roads in the city and elsewhere in the Valley, making it difficult for motorists to drive in the early hours. However, the situation may improve as the Valley is experiencing unusual winter sunshine.The Met Department has forecast that a western disturbance will affect the Union territory for a few days from January 22.Kashmir is currently under the grip of ‘Chillai-Kalan’ — the 40-day harshest winter period when cold wave grips the region and temperature drops considerably, leading to freezing of water bodies, including the famous Dal Lake here as well as water supply lines in several parts of the Valley.The chances of snowfall are the maximum during this period and most areas, especially in the higher reaches, receive heavy snowfall.While ‘Chillai-Kalan’, which begins on December 21, will end on January 31, the cold wave will continue even after that in Kashmir, with a 20-day-long ‘Chillai-Khurd’ (small cold) and 10-day-long ‘Chillai-Bachha’ (baby cold). — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/mercury-dips-across-kashmir-valley-201853
