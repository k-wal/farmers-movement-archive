17-03-2021||Srinagar||Former J-K deputy CM Baig joins Peoples Conference||Jammu and Kashmir's former Deputy Chief Minister and former PDP leader Muzaffar Hussain Baig on Wednesday joined the Sajad Lone-led People's Conference.Lone, accompanied by senior party leaders, visited Baig at his residence and held discussions on a range of issues faced by the people of J&K.In a statement, the People's Conference said Lone, on this occasion, invited Baig to return home and be a part of the party from where he started his political career."He impressed upon Baig that the need of the hour is that people like him should share their experience, expertise, knowledge and political wisdom for the betterment of J&K," it said.Baig, as per the statement, "expressed his pleasure in being able to be a part of the party from where he started his political career" and that he was pleased to be part of the party which he joined as a youth and was groomed by its chief, Abdul Gani Lone."Today he has the opportunity to yet again groom leaders like Sajad Lone and many others to take up leadership role in the future times to come," it added.The statement quoted Baig as saying that apart from many problems plaguing J&K, one of the biggest is the quality of leadership, and quite often he found that the leaders did not have the requisite exposure or the capacity to be able to contribute at a scale and at a level that they are supposed to do. He expressed his great desire to contribute by grooming youngsters and building their capacity to a level where they can rise up to the occasion for the people of J&K. 17-03-2021||New Delhi||MHA says terror incidents in J&K on decline, but cross-border firing increased||The central government said on Wednesday that there had been a significant decline in the terror incidents in Jammu and Kashmir in the last three years but instances of cross border firing from Pakistan have seen abrupt increase.Responding to a question in Rajya Sabha Union Minister of State (MoS) for Home G Kishen Reddy said, “During the past three years, there has been a significant decline in terror incidents and attempts of infiltration from across the border in Jammu and Kashmir. However, the incidents of shelling from across the border have increased during the past three years”.Giving the details of decreasing number of terror incidents in the Union Territory (UT), the Minister said, they have come down from 614 in 2018 to 244 in 2020. He went on to ad that killings of civilians have come down from 39 in 2018 to 37 in 2020 and deaths of security personnel from 91in 2018 to 62 in 2020.Reddy, however, contended that instances of shelling from Pakistan increased from 2,140 in 2018 to 5,133 in 2020.The Minister, while responding to another question relating to supply of weapons to infiltrators through drones, said, “Certain instances of supply of weapons via drones from across the Pakistan border have come to the notice of security and law enforcement agencies.”Reddy, however, asserted that the government had been taking various steps to meet such challenges, which include round the clock effective surveillance, streamlining the intelligence set up, capacity building of forces, patrolling on the borders along with sensitisation of local population in border areas, establishment of observation posts, border fencing, flood lighting, and use of modern and hi-tech surveillance equipments, etc.||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/mha-says-terror-incidents-in-jk-on-decline-but-cross-border-firing-increased-226579

17-03-2021||Jammu||Pak intruder shot along IB in Samba||A Pakistani intruder was gunned down by the BSF along the International Border (IB) in Samba district on Tuesday. This is the third such incident since November last year, a BSF spokesperson said.The incident took place in Ramgarh sector. “At about 3.55 pm, BSF troops observed suspicious movement of an intruder aggressively moving towards wild growth (sarkanda) while approaching fence from the side of Pakistani village Lehri Kalan and crossing the IB into the Indian territory between Border Post No 12/5 and 12/6. BSF men challenged him but he tried to escape into the Pakistan territory after which he was fired upon,” the spokesperson said.The body has been handed over to the Ramgarh police station.Officials said the deceased was in possession of Rs 200 in Pakistani currency and his physical appearance suggested that he was not mentally fit.His body is likely to be handed over to Pakistani rangers at a flag meeting. — PTIThird such case in four months||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/pak-intruder-shot-along-ib-in-samba-226181

17-03-2021||---||32.31 lakh domicile certificates issued||New Delhi, March 16The government today said a total of 32.31 lakh people in J&K had been given domicile certificates while 2.15 lakh applications have been rejected.In response to a written question in the Lok Sabha, Union Minister of State for Home G Kishan Reddy said, as per the information provided by the Union Territory (UT) government of J&K, a total of 35,44,938 applications for issuance of domicile certificates had been received as on December 31, 2020, out of which 32,31,353 applicants got domicile certificates.
