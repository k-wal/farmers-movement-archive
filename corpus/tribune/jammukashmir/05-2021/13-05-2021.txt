13-05-2021||New Delhi||India, Pakistan armies exchange sweets on Eid||Less than three months after Indian Army and Pakistan Army agreed to hold the ceasefire along the Line of Control (LOC), the two armies on Thursday exchanged sweets to mark Eid-ul-Fitr.The decision to agree to the 2003 ceasefire agreement was announced on February 26.Indian Army and Pakistan Army celebrated Eid-ul-Fitr on the LoC at Poonch-Rawalakot crossing point and Mendhar-Hotspring crossing point in Poonch district of Jammu & Kashmir, a spokesperson of the Northern Command of the Indian Army said.Sweets and compliments were exchanged by the representatives of both the armies in an atmosphere of bonhomie and festivities.   The ceremony is seen as a confidence building measure in the backdrop of recently agreed ceasefire between both the countries.The gesture was appreciated by both the armies and expected to promote goodwill and mutual trust, said the spokesperson.||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/india-pakistan-armies-exchange-sweets-on-eid-252333

13-05-2021||Srinagar||Day after Eid rush, curfew-like restrictions across Valley||A day after several areas in Kashmir witnessed a huge rush of people ahead of Eid festival, curfew-like restrictions were imposed in the Valley on Wednesday to contain an alarming surge in Covid cases in the Union Territory, officials said.Security forces have sealed vital roads and erected barricades at many places in the city and other district headquarters to stop unnecessary movement of people.Markets are shut and public transport is off the roads. Movement of only those engaged in essential services and in cases of emergencies is being allowed. The officials said restrictions were tightened on Wednesday in the wake of crowds gathering at several places in the city and many other areas of the Valley for Eid shopping.Massive traffic jams were witnessed on Tuesday in several parts of the city, including Lal Chowk, with people thronging the markets.On April 29, the Jammu and Kashmir administration imposed a curfew in 11 districts in a bid to tackle the surge in cases, which was subsequently extended to all 20 districts the next day.The curfew, which was supposed to end at 7 am on May 3, was extended in the twin capitals of Jammu and Srinagar, besides Budgam and Baramulla till May 6.Meanwhile, J&K recorded 4,509 fresh Covid cases, taking the tally to 2,29,407, while 65 deaths in the past 24 hours pushed the toll 2,912 in the Union Territory, officials said.The number of active cases has reached 51,542 in the UT, while 1,74,953 patients have recovered so far.In Ladakh, 135 fresh cases appeared, taking the tally to 15,564, while 130 more people recovered. The active cases in the Union Territory have now risen to 1,452, including 1,212 in Leh district and 240 in Kargil district.
