20-05-2021||Jammu||Army saves 2 persons from drowning in Kashmir||The Army rescued two people from drowning in the JCB vehicle that had got washed away in Marua River near Nawapachi village in Marwah valley of Kishtwar district of Jammu and Kashmir, army said on Thursday.Army said two civilians identified as Sajid Ahmad Mir, the driver, and Mushtaq Ahmad got stuck in the JCB when it got submerged while working on the bank and in the fast current. The vehicle had got washed away for around 500 meters."On receipt of information from locals, an Army column from Nawapachi post immediately rushed towards the site of the accident. Jumping into the river in the fast flowing water, Indian Army along with Jammu and Kashmir Police and locals successfully took out the civilians from the submerged JCB vehicle with the help of ropes in a 90 minute long difficult rescue mission," army said."Prompt action of troops in the rescue operation saved the lives of two civilians." The intruder got injured in firing along the International Border (IB) in Samba sector.Officials in the border guarding force said the security personnel opened fire at the person while he was trying to sneak into the Indian side of the border near Samba sector. The action resulted in a bullet injury to the intruder.According to sources, upon noticing the suspicious movement of the intruder, security men challenged him.
