26-05-2021||Jammu||66% above 45 years vaccinated in J&K||Nearly 66% people above 45 years of age have been administered Covid vaccine so far in J&K, top officials informed at a high-level meeting chaired by Union Home Secretary Ajay Kumar Bhalla to review the Covid management across all the union territories.J&K is among the leading regions in the country in vaccination of above 45-year age group as it has vaccinated 66% of its eligible population which is well above the national average of 32%, officials said.Four districts — Ganderbal, Jammu, Samba and Shopian — have achieved 100% coverage in this category, while the remaining districts continue with promising progress, they added. To accelerate the vaccination in the 18-45 age group, it was requested in the meeting that J&K be provided with a sustained supply of vaccines in the coming months, the officials said.The Union Home Secretary cautioned the UTs about the surge in Covid cases and asked them to remain vigilant about the situation.Advising the UTs to learn from their experiences, he asked them to keep pace with testing and vaccination rates, besides enforcing Covid appropriate behaviour and strengthening the infrastructure.At the outset, the Union Secretary, Health and Family Welfare, gave a presentation on the prevailing situation in the UTs. It was mentioned that J&K was performing reasonably well in mitigating the second wave of Covid with 3,946 cases per million population being reported over the last two weeks. — PTIL-G sets time frame for more infra||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/66-above-45-years-vaccinated-in-jk-258483
26-05-2021||Jammu||3 incubation facilities sanctioned||The Central Government has sanctioned three common incubation facility centres worth Rs 7.81 crore in J&K, a senior government official said on Tuesday. The centres would be set up at Anantnag, Baramulla and Jammu, said Navin Kumar Choudhary, Principal Secretary, Agriculture Production and Farmers’ Welfare Department. He termed these units as a milestone towards processing and marketing of agriculture business in the UT. Choudhary said the first incubation facility centre would be established in Anantnag with an estimated cost of Rs 2.70 crore. — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/3-incubation-facilities-sanctioned-258484
