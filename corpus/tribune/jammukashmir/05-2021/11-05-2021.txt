11-05-2021||Jammu||Covid lockdown in Leh extended till May 17||The Ladakh administration today extended till May 17 the curfew imposed in Leh to curb the spread of Covid-19. The curfew was earlier scheduled to end on Monday. The UT of J&K had announced lockdown extention a day before. Leh District Disaster Management Authority (DDMA) Chairman Shrikant Suse said stringent action would be taken against violators.  Shops selling essential items, including grocery, meat, chicken, dairy products, vegetables and bakeries, would open from 8 am to 1 pm daily, he said. Essential supplies would be made available to people living at far-off places that didn’t have such stores, he added. Stressing that people were exploiting relaxation and exemptions, he said the movement of vehicles would not be allowed. The DDMA office would provide vehicle movement passes on need basis for emergencies, he added. Suse requested people to not spread rumours and appealed to them to comply with the standard operating procedure. The restrictions, he said, would help health employees get a “much-needed” break. He also sought feedback from the head of departments regarding the rosters prepared for government employees. — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/covid-lockdown-in-leh-extended-till-may-17-250980
