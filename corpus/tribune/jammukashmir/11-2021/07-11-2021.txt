07-11-2021||Jammu||Forces comb Khabla forest as anti-terror ops extended||The main road connecting Rajouri to Thanamandi was briefly closed for traffic on Saturday as security forces extended the ongoing search for a group of terrorists to the Khabla forest following information about their suspected movement in the area, officials said.No success yetThe counter-insurgency operation in Surankote and Mendhar forest in Poonch district and Thanamandi in Rajouri district entered the 27th day on Saturday.While the operation to track down terrorists in the forest belt of Poonch-Rajouri was underway, the forces received information about the suspected movement of terrorists in the vast forest area of Khabla along the Mughal Road in the early hours of Saturday, officials said.An operation was jointly launched by the Army and the police in the forest area of Khabla but there was no contact with the suspected terrorists, they said.In view of the operation, the traffic on the Rajouri-Thanamandi road was suspended as a precautionary measure for the safety of commuters, officials said.Nine soldiers, including two JCOs, were killed in two separate encounters with the terrorists on the first day of the joint search operation in Surankote forest on October 11 and in Mendhar forest on October 14.A Pakistani terrorist, Zia Mustafa, who was shifted from Kot Bhalwal Central Jail, Jammu to Mendhar on police remand for questioning in connection with the operation, was killed when the security personnel accompanying him to identify a hideout in the Bhatti Durrian forest in Mendhar came under fire from the hiding terrorists on October 24. — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/forces-comb-khabla-forest-as-anti-terror-ops-extended-334914
07-11-2021||Srinagar||4 held, dummy weapon seized in Budgam||Four people were arrested while a dummy weapon was seized in connection with an extortion case in Budgam district, the police said.The police received a complaint on August 23 this year from Tawseef Ahmad Bhat, a resident of Rawatpora Khag, alleging that he and his neighbour Saqib Ahmed Dar were stopped by some masked persons on their way home in the evening a day earlier.They were shown a weapon and thrashed, while Bhat was robbed of Rs 1.5 lakh, a police spokesperson said.Later, Feroz Ahmad Wani of Pethzanigam, was questioned and he confessed his involvement, the spokesperson said. On Wani’s disclosure, his two associates Asif Ahmad Ahanger of Bonzanigam and Bilal Ahmad Malik from Pethzanigam were arrested and a dummy AK-47 weapon, Rs 33,300 in cash and three face covers seized. — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/4-held-dummy-weapon-seized-in-budgam-334915

07-11-2021||Srinagar||Jammu and Kashmir records 151  fresh Covid cases||Jammu and Kashmir recorded 151 fresh Covid cases, taking the infection tally to 3,32,911 on Saturday, while no new fatality was reported in the past 24 hours.Of the fresh cases, 16 were from Jammu division and 135 from Kashmir. Srinagar district recorded the highest of 79 cases, followed by 14 in Baramulla. There are 1,091 active cases in the UT, while 3,27,380 people have recovered from the infection, the officials said. The death toll due to the virus was 4,440 as no new fatality was reported in the past 24 hours.Meanwhile, the officials said there are 49 confirmed cases of mucormycosis (black fungus) in the Union Territory as no fresh case was reported since Friday evening. — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/j-k/jammu-and-kashmir-records-151-fresh-covid-cases-334917
