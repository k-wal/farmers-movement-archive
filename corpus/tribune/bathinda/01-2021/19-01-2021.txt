19-01-2021||Bathinda||BKU activists lay siege to DC office in Bathinda||Responding to the call given by the Sanyukt Kisan Morcha under the ongoing farmers’ agitation, women activists of the BKU (Sidhupur) today protested outside the Deputy Commissioner’s office here.In a show of strength, women conveyed the message that the role of women in half of the world’s population was no less than that of men in any field. He said whenever rights’ struggles were or would be waged in any corner of the world, ‘nari shakti’ would promptly demonstrate its existence.Accusing the central government of adopting a dictatorial attitude, the activists urged it to give up its stubbornness and address the demands of the farmers on a priority.Farmer leader Angrej Singh Kalyan said the Modi government was testing the patience of the farmers through repression. He said they would continue to persevere till their objective was realised. — TNS||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/bathinda/bku-activists-lay-siege-to-dc-office-in-bathinda-200293
19-01-2021||---||One more farmer dies in Bathinda||Muktsar, January 18Parminder Singh, 22, a farmer from Aulakh village who had returned from the Tikri border a few days ago, today died at a private hospital in Bathinda.Villagers said the deceased farmer had gone to the Delhi border on January 8. He was taken ill while protesting at the Tikri border. On his return, he was first taken to a hospital in Muktsar and then shifted to a Bathinda hospital where he breathed his last.The deceased is survived by his brother, father and mother. Parminder’s family owns nearly three acres and owes money to private lenders as well.||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/bathinda/one-more-farmer-dies-in-bathinda-200314
