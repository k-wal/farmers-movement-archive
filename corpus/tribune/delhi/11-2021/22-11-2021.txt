22-11-2021||New Delhi||Delhi govt lifts ban on construction and demolition activities: Environment Minister Gopal Rai||The Delhi government has lifted the ban on construction and demolition activities in view of an improvement in the air quality, Environment Minister Gopal Rai said on Monday.Rai said the government would take a call on the reopening of schools, and work-from-home for government staff on November 24.“We will also discuss if CNG-operated trucks carrying non-essential items can be allowed to enter Delhi,” he said.The Delhi government on Sunday night extended the ban on the entry of trucks carrying non-essential items and work-from-home for its employees till November 26 to combat air pollution and minimise its health effects. 22-11-2021||New Delhi||Air quality ‘very poor’, Delhi schools to remain closed||Schools in Delhi will remain closed for physical classes till further orders while online classes and board exams will continue, the Directorate of Education (DoE) announced on Sunday in view of the prevailing air quality situation.Delhi extends ban on entry of trucksNew Delhi: The Delhi Government on Sunday extended the ban on the entry of trucks carrying non-essential items into the city and work from home for its employees till November 26 to combat air pollution. PTIThe air quality remained “very poor” on Sunday morning, the authorities here said. The city recorded its air quality index (AQI) at 382 at 9 am. The 24-hour average AQI was 374 on Saturday.“The environment department has directed to close all schools with immediate effect till further orders of the Commission for Air Quality Management in NCR and adjoining areas. Therefore, all government and private schools will remain closed till further orders” Additional Director of Education Rita Sharma said.“However, online teaching learning activities and the examinations for board classes will be conducted as per guidelines issued earlier,” the official said.The Delhi Government had on November 13 announced closure of schools and other educational institutions in view of the deteriorating air quality in Delhi.Schools had reopened for all classes from November 1, after nearly 19 months of closure due to Covid-19. — PTI||https://www.tribuneindia.com/news/delhi/air-quality-very-poor-delhi-schools-to-remain-closed-341251
