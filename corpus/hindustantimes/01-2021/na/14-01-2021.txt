14-01-2021||---||LIVE| Don't believe in rumours being spread to defame farm stir, says BKU leader||The farmers’ agitation against the central government’s three contentious laws has entered day 50. The ninth round of talks between the farmers’ unions and the government will take place tomorrow. Eight rounds of talks have been held between the two sides which remained inconclusive. Ahead of Friday’s meeting, Union agriculture minister Narendra Singh Tomar said on Thursday that the government was ready to hold discussions with an open mind.As the Supreme Court had stayed the implementation of the farm laws and formed a four-member panel to hear the protesting farmers as well as the government, Bharatiya Kisan Union chief Bhupinder Singh Mann- who was a member of the panel- decided to step down from this position when BKU’s Punjab unit decided to disassociate itself from him.“As a farmer myself and a union leader, in view of the prevailing sentiments and apprehensions amongst the farm unions and the public in general, I am ready to sacrifice any position offered or given to me so as to not compromise the interests of Punjab and farmers of the country,” Maan had said, according to news agency PTI.Meanwhile, Congress leader Rahul Gandhi once again hit out at the Centre regarding the farm laws and said that the government would be forced to take back the laws.
