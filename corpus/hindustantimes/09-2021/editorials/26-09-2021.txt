26-09-2021||Editorials||The right to sit must be the beginning||On September 13, the Tamil Nadu (TN) assembly passed the amended Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act, 1947, making it mandatory for shops, storefronts, and commercial establishments to provide employees with seating facilities. TN is the second state to do so after Kerala. With most establishments having no chairs or stools for salespersons who work for over 10 hours a day, often with no toilet or tea breaks, workers developed various physical ailments (and most of the workers are women). These rules defy every tenet of labour rights and human dignity, and are often compounded by paltry wages and scant benefits.
